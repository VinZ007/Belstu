Season 2023

The 2023 Formula 1 season was defined by Max Verstappen's unprecedented dominance, who won 19 out of 22 races and secured his third consecutive World Drivers' Championship title for Red Bull Racing, which also won the Constructors' Championship.

Notable elements included the addition of the Las Vegas Grand Prix, a new event on the calendar, and significant strides in Formula 1's sustainability efforts, such as the introduction of the first Impact Report and the use of biofuel-powered trucks for European logistics.

Notable Races & Events

  • Las Vegas Grand Prix: A new and highly anticipated addition to the calendar.
  • Singapore Grand Prix: A costly strategy error by Mercedes highlighted areas for improvement despite strong performance.
  • Qatar Grand Prix: Verstappen clinched his third Drivers' Championship title at this race.

Season 2024

The 2024 Formula 1 season was a year of unexpected drama, featuring a record seven different winners, a thrilling constructor's championship battle that went down to the final race, and impressive performances from Lando Norris and McLaren, who emerged as the biggest challengers to Max Verstappen's dominance.

Verstappen ultimately secured his fourth consecutive Drivers' Championship, though less decisively than in previous years, while sustainability efforts focused on reducing the sport's carbon footprint through increased use of Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) and optimizing the race calendar.

On-Track Highlights

  • Unprecedented Driver Diversity: The 2024 season saw a record seven different drivers win a Grand Prix, a feat not seen since 2012.
  • McLaren's Breakthrough: Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri were instrumental in McLaren's rise, with both drivers securing victories and podium finishes.
  • Verstappen's Fourth Title: Max Verstappen secured his fourth consecutive Drivers' Championship, although the competition was tighter than in 2023, with his defining win being a remarkable recovery drive in wet conditions at São Paulo.
  • Hamilton's Return: Lewis Hamilton ended a long drought, securing his first victory in 945 days at the British Grand Prix, his ninth win at Silverstone.
  • Leclerc's Monaco Joy: Charles Leclerc celebrated a popular home victory in Monaco, marking a significant personal achievement.
  • Thrilling Constructor Battle: The World Constructors' Championship went down to the wire, with a fierce battle between the leading teams going into the final race.

Latest News

After taking the fastest pole position lap in Formula One history, Verstappen converted it into his and Red Bull's third win of the season at the Italian Grand Prix, ahead of Norris and Piastri. Controversial late-stage team orders were given to the latter, following his teammate's slow pit stop resulting in the former overtaking him for second.
